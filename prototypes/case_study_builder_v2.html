Stage 1 of 5
Research Question
GPS Requirement

A GPS-compliant proof argument begins with a specific, answerable research question. It must name the subject, the claim being tested, and the time and place. Vague questions produce vague conclusions.

Subject
Jacob SINGER  /  Yankel SPRINGER
b. 3 December 1895, Lechowitz, Volhynia, Russian Empire  ·  d. 2 February 1981, Oakland, Bergen County, New Jersey
Why This Question Requires Proof

The subject appears in all U.S. records as Jacob Singer or variants (Jack, Jake). He appears in the 1907 passenger manifest as Yankel Springer, under his family's original surname. No single document explicitly bridges the two names. This case study constructs that proof from the available record.

Evidence Explained Citation Standard

Stage 3 of 5
Evidence Chain
GPS Requirement

No single link proves identity alone. The chain as a whole must produce a preponderance of evidence. Each link is assessed for weight and carries its footnote reference. Gaps must be acknowledged.

Identified Gap -- Reasonably Exhaustive Search Not Yet Complete

No document explicitly bridges the two surnames. The 1910 federal census has not been searched -- this is the primary gap in the 1907-1915 chronological chain. The Seaman's Protection Certificate (NARA RG 41) has been cited but not yet retrieved. The Volhynia metrical books for Lachovitz have not been searched. These searches must be completed or their absence explained before this argument may be considered final.

Stage 4 of 5
Conflict Analysis
GPS Requirement

GPS requires that conflicting evidence be identified and resolved before a proof argument can be written. Every conflict needs a written analysis. You cannot proceed by ignoring a conflict -- doing so invalidates the proof.

Stage 5 of 5
Proof Argument
GPS Requirement

The proof argument is a narrative synthesis. Every factual claim carries an inline footnote keyed to a specific source. Conflicts are addressed in the argument, not ignored. Gaps are acknowledged. The conclusion is clearly stated and follows from the evidence.
