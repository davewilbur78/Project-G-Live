2. CONNIE KNOX / GENEALOGYTV -- Research Notes Method
   Dave referenced GenealogyTV (Constance Henley Knox) and specifically her
   Knox Research Notes Method as something he wants integrated into the platform.

   What the Knox method is:
   - Research Notes are person-centric, chronological, living documents
   - Contain ONLY positive findings (facts, sources, conclusions) for ONE ancestor
   - Explicitly NOT the Research Log (which tracks everywhere you looked)
   - Called by Connie "the most important document in your files"
   - Becomes the cumulative life portrait of an individual
   - Basis for future reports, books, and proof arguments
   - Knox Research Notes Method is now formally named and taught as a standalone
     course in the GenealogyTV Academy
   - She also teaches: Trifecta Strategy (search methodology), Genealogy Master
     Plan (overall research framework), and distinguishes Research Notes from
     Research Logs clearly

   Key architectural insight: Research Notes attach to the PERSON, not to a
   project or case study. Julia Klein's notes are Julia Klein's notes regardless
   of whether you're working on her as personal research, a group project, or
   a client matter. The project determines context; the notes travel with the person.

   Dave's honest self-assessment: he hasn't started these notes despite wanting
   to, because he doesn't want to create and manage files. He needs the platform
   to do it for him -- AI-assisted generation from existing structured data,
   with the notes flowing both ways (generated from what's in the system, and
   feeding back into it).

   Connection to existing modules: Research Notes are distinct from and complementary
   to Module 3 (Research Log), Module 10 (Case Study Builder), and Module 9
   (Research Report Writer). They are the living working document. The others
   are formal outputs.

   Connection to persons table: Research Notes should be attached to the persons
   table record. The person profile we've begun is the natural home.
