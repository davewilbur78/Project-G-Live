2026-05-14 UTC -- AI ICEBREAKER on person detail page.
  When you land on a person's page, an AI-generated conversation starter surfaces.
  Not a summary -- a prompt. Something that orients the researcher and suggests
  a direction. Examples: "Aaron has 19 sourced events but no census coverage
  between 1910 and 1920 -- that gap is worth investigating."
  Or: "Three of Stanley's sources conflict on birth year. The Source Conflict
  Resolver has an open case."
  Or simply: a single compelling observation derived from what the data actually shows.
  Engine: GRA + research-assistant-v8. Generated on page load. Cacheable.
  This is the platform talking to the researcher, not the other way around.
  Connie Knox equivalent: the red items she writes in research notes.
  This platform surfaces them automatically.

2026-05-14 UTC -- MAPS on the person detail page.
  Address-as-Evidence is already a foundational principle (established 2026-05-10).
  The addresses table has lat/lng fields. The timeline_events table has place_name.
  The person detail page should show a map of the person's geographic life story --
  pins for every place they appear in the record, connected in chronological order.
  This is not decorative. It is a research tool. A person who appears in
  New York in 1900 and Connecticut in 1910 has a migration story. The map sees it
  instantly. Name search never would.
  Map panel lives alongside or below the timeline.
  Geocoding step needed: address -> lat/lng. Already flagged in backlog.
  In v1: show places where lat/lng is already populated. Geocoding comes later.
  The addresses table lat/lng fields were designed for exactly this moment.
